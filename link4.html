You simply cannot do Christopher Nolan's Michael Mann tone without Nolan, and you can't beat Heath. So you veer left. Take a sharp turn back into pop art cartoonish absurdism a la Schumacher at his best (read: Batman Forever). At least, that was the intent. More cartoonish than Mark Hamill, and with no substance to support it.
